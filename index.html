Hello, this is Luca Newman, I am 14 years old. My father is a piano technician who builds pianos. Although he can play the piano well enough to perform in public, he cannot read music, so how I learned the piano at first was by ear and we were enjoying playing together everyday. Just before turning eight years old, I had my first piano lesson to learn to read music. I was fascinated by the patterns of notes and bought the Chopin Etudes for myself which were my favourite compositions listening to the CD. I realised that the classical music that I loved listening to on the radio were all in the books. My parents bought me many sheet music books whenever I found pieces that I liked and I read them like a story book whenever I had time everyday. Around that time, I started composing too.


I applied to the Purcell Music school when I was ten and passed the first studies in both the keyboard and composition departments. On the open day at the school, I showed my composition notebooks to students who were already studying at the school. One of them was surprised and said "Are you Mozart?!" as I could compose without using any instruments but just in my head. I was thrilled to be chosen to compose an orchestral piece for the school orchestral last year and the piece is was performed in March.


I have entered six international piano competitions and was awarded second prize twice, and four first prizes including the Piano Talents International Competition in Milan in June 2023. I have performed some public concerts and had my solo recital in Romania when I was 12 which was broadcast on the National radio. I also performed my first piano concerto (Mendelssohn piano concerto no 1 opus 25) with a full orchestra in 2023.
